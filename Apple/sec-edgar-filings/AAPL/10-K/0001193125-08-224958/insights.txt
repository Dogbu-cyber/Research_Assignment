Summary:
Apple Inc. appears to be in strong financial health based on the financial data provided in the report. The company reported robust net income of $4,834 million and operating income of $6,275 million for the fiscal year ended September 27, 2008. These figures represent significant increases from the prior year, with net income growing by 38% and operating income increasing by 42%. 

The company's gross margin percentage remained relatively stable at 34.3% in fiscal 2008, compared to 34.0% in the prior year. This indicates that Apple has been able to maintain its profitability despite the competitive nature of the markets in which it operates.

Apple's cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments totaled $24,490 million as of September 27, 2008, up 59% from the prior year. This strong liquidity position provides the company with ample resources to fund its operations, invest in research and development, and pursue strategic initiatives.

Furthermore, Apple's return on equity (net income divided by shareholders' equity) was 23% in fiscal 2008, suggesting that the company is effectively utilizing its capital to generate profits for shareholders.

Overall, the financial data presented in the report demonstrates that Apple is in a robust financial position, with strong profitability, liquidity, and capital efficiency. The company's ability to consistently grow its top and bottom lines, while maintaining healthy margins, is a testament to its operational excellence and the strength of its product portfolio.
