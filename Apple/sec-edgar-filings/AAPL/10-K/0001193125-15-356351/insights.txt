Summary: Apple Inc. appears to be in strong financial health based on the financial data provided in the report.

The company reported a net income of $53,394 million, which is a significant increase from the previous year's net income of $39,510 million. This indicates that Apple is generating substantial profits and is effectively converting its revenue into net income.

The company's operating income of $71,230 million is also very strong, reflecting the company's ability to generate substantial income from its core business operations. The operating income has increased from the previous year's $52,503 million, suggesting that Apple is effectively managing its expenses and maintaining a high level of profitability.

The report also highlights Apple's strong cash position, with $205,666 million in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities as of September 26, 2015. This large cash reserve provides the company with financial flexibility and the ability to invest in future growth opportunities.

Additionally, the company's capital return program, which includes share repurchases and dividend payments, demonstrates its commitment to returning value to shareholders. The report indicates that Apple has spent $143,510 million on this program since its inception in 2012, further strengthening its financial position.

Overall, the financial data presented in the report suggests that Apple is in a robust financial position, with strong profitability, a healthy cash reserve, and a commitment to shareholder value creation. These factors indicate that the company is well-positioned to continue its growth and success in the future.
