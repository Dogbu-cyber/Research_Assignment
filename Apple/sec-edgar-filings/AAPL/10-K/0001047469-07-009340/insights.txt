Summary: Based on the financial information provided in the report, Apple Inc. appears to be in strong financial health.

The company reported a net income of $3,496 million and an operating income of $4,409 million for the fiscal year 2007. These positive figures indicate that Apple is generating substantial profits from its operations and is effectively managing its costs and expenses.

The company's gross margin percentage increased significantly from 29.0% in 2006 to 34.0% in 2007, suggesting improved profitability and cost management. This can be attributed to more favorable costs on certain components, higher overall revenue providing better leverage on fixed production costs, and a higher percentage of revenue from the company's direct sales channels.

Apple's cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments balance grew from $10,110 million in 2006 to $15,386 million in 2007, indicating a strong liquidity position and the ability to fund future growth and investments. The company's operating cash flow of $5,470 million in 2007 further demonstrates its ability to generate substantial cash from its operations.

Overall, the financial data presented in the report suggests that Apple is in a robust financial position, with strong profitability, liquidity, and cash generation capabilities. The company's consistent growth in revenue, net income, and operating income, coupled with its healthy balance sheet, indicate that Apple is well-positioned to continue its successful performance and pursue strategic initiatives to drive future growth.
