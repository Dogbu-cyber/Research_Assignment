Net Income: $21,863 million

Operating Income: $26,764 million

Summary:
Microsoft's financial health appears strong based on the data provided in the report. The company reported robust net income of $21,863 million and operating income of $26,764 million for the fiscal year 2013. These figures represent a 29% increase in net income and a 23% increase in operating income compared to the prior fiscal year.

The company's revenue grew 6% year-over-year, reaching $77,849 million, driven by higher revenue from the Server and Tools segment as well as new product and service offerings such as Windows 8, Surface, and the new Office suite. This revenue growth, coupled with effective cost management, contributed to the strong profitability metrics.

Microsoft's balance sheet also appears healthy, with $77,022 million in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments as of June 30, 2013, up from $63,040 million the prior year. This substantial cash position provides the company with ample financial flexibility to fund operations, invest in growth initiatives, and return capital to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases.

The report also highlights Microsoft's focus on innovation and expanding its product and service offerings, which is crucial for maintaining its competitive position in the rapidly evolving technology industry. The successful launch of new products like Windows 8 and Surface, as well as the continued growth of the Server and Tools and Microsoft Business Division segments, demonstrate the company's ability to adapt to changing market dynamics.

Overall, the financial data presented in the report suggests that Microsoft is in a strong financial position, with robust profitability, a solid balance sheet, and a commitment to innovation and growth, which bodes well for the company's long-term prospects.
