Net Income: $9,421

Operating Income: $10,937

Summary: Microsoft's overall financial health appears to be strong based on the data provided in the report.

The company reported a net income of $9,421 million in fiscal year 2000, which represents a significant increase from the previous two fiscal years ($4,490 million in 1998 and $7,785 million in 1999). This growth in net income suggests that Microsoft is generating substantial profits and is effectively managing its operations and costs.

The company's operating income also grew steadily, reaching $10,937 million in fiscal year 2000, up from $6,414 million in 1998 and $9,928 million in 1999. This indicates that Microsoft is generating strong operating cash flows and is able to cover its operating expenses.

Microsoft's balance sheet appears to be in a healthy condition as well. The company had $23,798 million in cash and short-term investments as of June 30, 2000, which provides ample liquidity and financial flexibility. Additionally, the company's stockholders' equity stood at $41,368 million, suggesting a strong capital structure.

Overall, the financial data presented in the report suggests that Microsoft is a financially robust company with a solid balance sheet, strong profitability, and the ability to generate substantial cash flows from its operations. These factors indicate that the company is well-positioned to continue its growth and expansion in the future.
