Net Income: $8,168 million

Operating Income: $9,034 million

Summary:
Microsoft's financial health appears strong based on the data provided in the report. The company reported robust net income of $8,168 million and operating income of $9,034 million for the fiscal year 2004. This represents a 8.5% increase in net income and a 5.3% decrease in operating income compared to the prior year.

The company's revenue grew 14.4% year-over-year to $36,835 million, indicating strong demand for Microsoft's products and services. Gross profit margin remained healthy at 81.8%, suggesting the company is able to maintain pricing power and control costs effectively.

Microsoft's balance sheet is also in a solid position, with $60,592 million in cash and short-term investments as of June 30, 2004, up from $49,048 million the prior year. This provides the company with ample liquidity and financial flexibility to fund operations, invest in growth initiatives, and return capital to shareholders.

The report also highlights Microsoft's commitment to shareholder value, with the board of directors approving a $30 billion share repurchase program and a $3 per share special dividend, subject to shareholder approval. These actions demonstrate the company's confidence in its long-term prospects and its willingness to deploy its substantial cash reserves to benefit shareholders.

Overall, the financial data presented in the report suggests that Microsoft is in a strong financial position, with solid revenue growth, healthy profitability, and a robust balance sheet. These factors indicate the company's ability to continue executing its strategic objectives and delivering value to shareholders.
