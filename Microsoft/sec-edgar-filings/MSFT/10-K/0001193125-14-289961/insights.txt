Operating Income: $27,759 million

Summary:
Microsoft's financial health appears strong based on the data provided in the report. The company reported robust net income of $22,074 million and operating income of $27,759 million for the fiscal year 2014. This represents a 1% increase in net income and a 4% increase in operating income compared to the prior fiscal year. 

The company's revenue grew 12% year-over-year, reaching $86,833 million, driven by higher revenue across both the Devices and Consumer and Commercial segments. Gross margin also increased 4% to $59,899 million, indicating the company's ability to maintain profitability despite higher costs.

Microsoft's balance sheet is healthy, with $85,709 million in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments as of June 30, 2014, up from $77,022 million the prior year. This strong liquidity position provides the company with financial flexibility to fund operations, invest in growth initiatives, and return capital to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases.

The company's return on equity of 24.6% and return on assets of 12.8% demonstrate efficient utilization of its capital and assets to generate profits. Additionally, Microsoft's debt-to-equity ratio of 0.23 suggests a conservative capital structure, with the majority of its financing coming from equity.

Overall, the financial data presented in the report indicates that Microsoft is in a robust financial position, with strong profitability, liquidity, and capital efficiency, positioning the company well to execute on its strategic priorities and create value for shareholders.
