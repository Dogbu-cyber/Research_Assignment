Net Income: $16,571

Operating Income: $35,058

Summary: Microsoft's financial health appears strong based on the data provided in the report. The company reported robust operating income of $35,058 million, a 21% increase from the prior year. Net income was $16,571 million, though this figure was impacted by a $13.7 billion net charge related to the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act. Excluding this one-time charge, adjusted net income grew 18% year-over-year to $30,267 million.

The company's revenue grew 14% to $110,360 million, driven by strong performance across its three reporting segments - Productivity and Business Processes, Intelligent Cloud, and More Personal Computing. Gross margin also increased 16%, indicating the company's ability to effectively manage costs.

Microsoft's balance sheet remains healthy, with $133.8 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments as of June 30, 2018. The company generated $43.9 billion in cash from operations during the fiscal year, up 11% year-over-year, demonstrating its ability to consistently convert earnings into cash flow.

Overall, the data suggests Microsoft is in a solid financial position, with strong profitability, revenue growth, and cash generation. The company's diversified business model and investments in key growth areas like cloud computing appear to be paying off, positioning it well for continued success.
