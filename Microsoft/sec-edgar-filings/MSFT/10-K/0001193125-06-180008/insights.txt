Net Income: $12,599 million

Operating Income: $16,472 million

Summary:
Microsoft's financial health appears strong based on the data provided in the report. The company reported robust net income of $12,599 million and operating income of $16,472 million for the fiscal year 2006. These figures represent a 2.8% increase in net income and a 13.1% increase in operating income compared to the prior fiscal year.

The company's revenue grew 11% year-over-year, driven by strong performance across multiple business segments, including Client, Server and Tools, and Information Worker. This revenue growth, coupled with effective cost management, contributed to the expansion in operating margins from 36.6% in fiscal year 2005 to 37.2% in fiscal year 2006.

Microsoft's balance sheet also appears healthy, with $34.16 billion in cash and short-term investments as of June 30, 2006. This strong liquidity position, along with the company's consistent profitability, suggests that Microsoft has the financial resources to fund its ongoing operations, research and development, and strategic initiatives.

Furthermore, Microsoft's return on equity (ROE) was 31.4% in fiscal year 2006, indicating efficient utilization of shareholders' capital. The company's ability to generate high returns on equity is a positive indicator of its overall financial health and management's effectiveness in deploying capital.

Overall, the data presented in the report suggests that Microsoft is in a solid financial position, with strong profitability, liquidity, and capital efficiency, which should enable the company to continue investing in growth opportunities and delivering value to its shareholders.
