Net Income: $39,240 million

Operating Income: $42,959 million

Summary:
Microsoft's financial health appears to be strong based on the data provided in the report. The company reported robust net income of $39,240 million and operating income of $42,959 million for the fiscal year 2019. This represents a significant increase of 137% in net income and 23% in operating income compared to the prior fiscal year.

The company's revenue grew by 14% year-over-year, driven by strong performance across all three of its reportable segments: Productivity and Business Processes, Intelligent Cloud, and More Personal Computing. The Intelligent Cloud segment, in particular, saw a 21% increase in revenue, while the Productivity and Business Processes and More Personal Computing segments grew by 15% and 8%, respectively.

Gross margin also improved by 15%, indicating that the company was able to effectively manage its costs and maintain profitability. The company's effective tax rate decreased from 54.6% in fiscal year 2018 to 10.2% in fiscal year 2019, primarily due to a $2.6 billion net income tax benefit related to intangible property transfers.

Microsoft's balance sheet remains strong, with $133.8 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments as of June 30, 2019. The company's long-term debt stood at $66.7 billion, suggesting a healthy financial position and the ability to fund future growth initiatives.

Overall, the data presented in the report suggests that Microsoft is in a robust financial position, with strong revenue growth, improved profitability, and a solid balance sheet. The company's diversified business model and successful execution across its key segments have contributed to its impressive financial performance.
