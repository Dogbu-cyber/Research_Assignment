Net Income: $7,829 million

Operating Income: $11,910 million

Summary:
Microsoft's financial health appears strong based on the data provided in the report. The company reported robust net income of $7,829 million and operating income of $11,910 million for the fiscal year 2002. This represents a consistent level of profitability, with net income remaining relatively stable over the past three years ($9,421 million in 2000, $7,346 million in 2001, and $7,829 million in 2002).

The company's balance sheet also reflects a solid financial position, with $38,652 million in cash and short-term investments as of June 30, 2002, up from $31,600 million the prior year. This substantial cash balance provides Microsoft with ample liquidity and financial flexibility to fund operations, invest in growth initiatives, and return capital to shareholders.

Additionally, the company's unearned revenue, which represents deferred income from volume licensing programs and undelivered elements, grew from $5,614 million in 2001 to $7,743 million in 2002. This indicates strong demand for Microsoft's products and services, and provides a stable revenue stream going forward.

Overall, the financial data suggests that Microsoft continues to maintain a healthy and profitable business model, with a strong balance sheet and ample cash reserves to support its strategic objectives. The company's consistent financial performance and solid liquidity position point to a robust financial health.
